About Me

I'm a product designer who thinks like an engineer and designs for humans.

My Journey

I'm a 26-year-old curious, trilingual lifelong student of design thinking, raised in an academic family of engineers. My path to product design has been anything but linear.

With a background in computer engineering, I spent years building systems at The Linux Foundation as a Google Summer of Code contributor and working with tech startups as a DevOps engineer. I built secure OTA update systems, architected cloud infrastructure, and automated deployment pipelines. The technical challenges were fascinating, but I found myself increasingly drawn to a different question: Why?

Why did users struggle with certain interfaces? Why did technically perfect products fail to gain adoption? Why were we building features that nobody asked for? These questions led me to pursue a Master's in Engineering Design with a specialization in Product Design at McMaster University.

Design Philosophy

"The best solutions emerge when you deeply understand not just what users want, but what they fear, and bridge that with what's technically possible."

I believe:

  • Empathy drives innovation. Understanding user fears and pain points is as important as understanding their desires.
  • Constraints spark creativity. Technical limitations, manufacturing realities, and budget constraints aren't barriers‚Äîthey're design parameters that lead to elegant solutions.
  • Process matters. Good design isn't about the final mockup‚Äîit's about the research, iteration, testing, and learning that gets you there.
  • Design is a team sport. The best work happens when designers, engineers, and stakeholders collaborate from day one.
